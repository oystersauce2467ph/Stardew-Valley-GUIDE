## 1. How Relationships Work

Every villager you can befriend has a **friendship meter** measured in **hearts**.

- **1 heart = 250 friendship points.**
- Most villagers cap at **10 hearts** (2,500 points).
- A **spouse or roommate** can reach **14 hearts** (3,750 points).
- Marriage candidates are **locked at 8 hearts** until you start dating them with a **bouquet** (see [Section 4](#4-romance-dating--marriage)).

You can see every meter on the **Social tab** of your menu. When you talk to someone, a colored dot shows your progress: blue (0–1), green (2–3), yellow (4–5), red (6–7), purple (8–9), and a **purple star** at 10+.

### Ways to GAIN friendship
| Action | Points |
| --- | --- |
| Talk to them once per day | **+20** |
| Give a **loved** gift | **+80** |
| Give a **liked** gift | **+45** |
| Give a **neutral** gift | **+20** |
| Complete a villager's item-delivery **quest** | **+150** |
| Give 2 gifts in one week (bonus on Sunday) | **+10** |
| Invite to the **Movie Theater** (loved / liked film) | **+200 / +100** |
| Buy them a snack at the Movie Theater (loved / liked) | **+50 / +25** |
| Kiss your spouse (first kiss of the day) | **+10** |

### Ways to LOSE friendship
| Action | Points |
| --- | --- |
| Give a **disliked** gift | **−20** |
| Give a **hated** gift | **−40** |
| Not greeting them (daily **decay** — see below) | small |
| Hitting them with the slingshot | **−30** |
| Digging a trash can while they watch (within 7 tiles) | **−25** (except **Linus**, who *gains* +5) |

### Friendship decay (use it or lose it)
If you don't talk to someone, their meter slowly drops each day — **unless it is already full**.

| Relationship | Decay rate | Decay stops at |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Not romanceable | −2 / day | 10 hearts |
| Marriage candidate (before bouquet) | −2 / day | 8 hearts |
| Dating (bouquet given) | −10 / day | 10 hearts |
| Married | −20 / day | **never** |

> **Beginner takeaway:** Once a non-romance villager is at **full 10 hearts**, you can stop visiting and they stay there. Spouses always decay, so keep talking to and kissing your spouse daily.

### Two helpful boosts
- **Friendship 101** (a book from the Bookseller): permanently increases **all** friendship gains by **+10%**.
- Reaching **2 hearts** unlocks a villager's bedroom, making them easier to find for talking and gifting.

## 2. Gift Point Values (the math)

You may give each villager **one gift per day**, up to **two gifts per week** (the week resets on **Sunday**). Birthdays are an exception — you can always give a birthday gift even after hitting the weekly limit.

Each gift's value depends on the villager's taste, multiplied by special-day bonuses:

| Taste | Normal | On Birthday (**×8**) | Winter Star gift (**×5**) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Love** | +80 | **+640** | +400 |
| **Like** | +45 | +360 | +225 |
| **Neutral** | +20 | +160 | +100 |
| **Dislike** | −20 | −160 | −100 |
| **Hate** | −40 | −320 | −200 |

**Item quality** further boosts *loved and liked* gifts (silver ×1.1, gold ×1.25, iridium ×1.5). Quality does **not** matter for neutral/disliked/hated gifts.

> **Golden rule:** A normal-quality **loved** gift always beats an iridium-quality **liked** gift. Prioritize loves.

> **Best single gift possible:** an iridium-quality loved item on a villager's **birthday** = **960 points** (nearly 4 hearts at once!). This is the single fastest way to build a relationship.

## 3. The Fastest Way to Max a Relationship

Follow this routine and you'll hit 10 hearts quickly:

1. **Give 2 loved gifts every week.** Two loved gifts = +160/week, plus the +10 two-gift bonus = **+170/week** per villager.
2. **Hammer birthdays.** A loved gift on a birthday is **+640** (×8). Save a loved item for each villager's birthday — it's worth eight normal gifts. (Stack quality for even more.)
3. **Talk every day.** +20/day adds up and also stops decay.
4. **Do their quests.** "Help Wanted" item-delivery quests on the board give **+150** each.
5. **Use the Movie Theater** (built after the Community Center / Joja route). Inviting someone to a film they love is **+200** in one outing.
6. **Stardrop Tea** is the secret weapon: it gives a **full heart (+250)**, ignores the 2-gift weekly limit, and you can give any number. (It's expensive/late-game but unbeatable for a final push.)

### A sample week to max one person fast
- **Sunday:** talk + give loved gift #1
- **Wednesday:** talk + give loved gift #2 → +10 bonus next Sunday
- **Every other day:** just talk (+20)
- **On their birthday:** give your best-quality loved gift (+640)

## 4. Romance, Dating & Marriage

**12 villagers** can be romanced and married — **6 bachelors** and **6 bachelorettes**.

### The romance steps
1. **Reach 8 hearts.** Marriage candidates are capped here until you show romantic interest.
2. **Give a [Bouquet](https://stardewvalleywiki.com/Bouquet)** (bought at **Pierre's General Store for 800g**, available once they're at 8 hearts). This makes you **boyfriend/girlfriend** and unlocks hearts 9 and 10.
3. **Reach 10 hearts.**
4. **Give a [Mermaid's Pendant](https://stardewvalleywiki.com/Mermaid%27s_Pendant)** (**5,000g** from the **Old Mariner**, who appears on the beach on rainy days after you're at 10 hearts with someone). This is a marriage proposal.
5. **Wedding** happens 3 days later. You also need an **upgraded farmhouse** (the first house upgrade adds a kitchen and nursery).

### Important notes for beginners
- **You can date multiple people at once** (give several bouquets) with no penalty — *until* you propose. After engagement/marriage, your partner may get **jealous**.
- **Breaking up:** a **[Wilted Bouquet](https://stardewvalleywiki.com/Wilted_Bouquet)** ends a relationship and drops that meter to 5 hearts.
- **Spouses get a 14-heart meter** after marriage and give you perks (helping with chores, a room in your house, etc.).
- **Spouse gifting:** you can gift your spouse **every day**, but the friendship change from gifts is **halved**, and spouse friendship **always decays** — so keep interacting daily.
- **[Krobus](https://stardewvalleywiki.com/Krobus)** can move in as a **roommate** (not a spouse) and also uses a 14-heart meter.

## 5. Universal Gift Tastes (your fallback cheat sheet)

When you don't know a specific villager's taste, these rules apply to **almost everyone**. Individual tastes (Section 6) override these.

### ❤️ Universal Loves (+80 to nearly everyone)
These are loved by all villagers but are hard to get:
- **Golden Pumpkin** (Spirit's Eve festival / Artifact Troves)
- **Magic Rock Candy** (Desert Trader / Geodes)
- **Pearl** (rare — Night Market, fishing chests, Blobfish fish pond)
- **Prismatic Shard** *(except **Haley**, who hates it)*
- **Rabbit's Foot** *(except **Penny**, who hates it)*
- **Stardrop Tea** — gives **+250** (a full heart!), ignores the weekly gift limit

### 👍 Universal Likes (+45 — easy and reliable)
- **All Artisan Goods** — wine, cheese, jelly, honey, etc. *(except **Oil** = dislike, **Void Mayonnaise** = hate)*
- **All Cooked Dishes** *(except **Fried Egg** & **Bread** = neutral; **Strange Bun** & **Seafoam Pudding** = hate)*
- **All Flowers** *(except **Poppy** = hate)*
- **All Foraged Minerals** *(except **Quartz**, which varies)*
- **All Fruit-Tree Fruit** — apple, apricot, cherry, orange, peach, pomegranate *(Banana & Mango follow each villager's normal fruit preference)*
- **All Gems** — emerald, aquamarine, ruby, amethyst, topaz, jade, diamond *(Prismatic Shard is a Love)*
- **All Vegetables** *(except **Hops**, **Tea Leaves**, **Wheat** = neutral; **Unmilled Rice** = dislike)*
- Plus: **Life Elixir, Maple Syrup, Piña Colada, Rainbow Shell, Treasure Chest**

### 😐 Universal Neutrals (+20)
All Books (except Price Catalogue), Bread, Coral, Duck Feather, Fried Egg, Hops, Mystic Syrup, Nautilus Shell, Roe, Squid Ink, Sweet Gem Berry, Tea Leaves, Truffle, Wheat, Wool.

### 👎 Universal Dislikes (−20)
Building materials (wood, stone, fiber, clay, hardwood, moss, battery packs), **all Artifacts**, bombs, crafted floors/paths, fences, fertilizer, **all Fish** *(except Carp & Snails = hate)*, geodes & geode minerals, **all Seeds/Saplings**, sprinklers, tackle, trinkets, fireworks, ore & metal bars; plus Cave Carrot, Driftwood, Field Snack, Jack-O-Lantern, Oak Resin, **Oil**, Unmilled Rice.

### 🚫 Universal Hates (−40 — never gift these)
**All Bait**, **all Fossils**, **all Monster Loot** *(except Solar/Void Essence = dislike)*, **all Trash**; plus Carp, Snail, **Poppy**, **Red Mushroom**, **Strange Bun**, **Seafoam Pudding**, **Void Mayonnaise**, Hay, ores (copper/iron), and similar low-value junk.
