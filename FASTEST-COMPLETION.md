# Stardew Valley — Fastest Path to Completion & Maxing All Tools (Beginner's Guide)

> **Game version:** Stardew Valley **1.6.15** (PC) / **1.6.15.1** (Xbox, PlayStation, Switch). Current latest version.
> **Source:** All costs, requirements, and mechanics verified against the official [Stardew Valley Wiki](https://stardewvalleywiki.com/). Content was rephrased for compliance with licensing restrictions.

This guide is the **shortest realistic route** for a beginner to "finish" Stardew Valley, starting from **Spring 1, Year 1**. It lays out every season step-by-step, tells you **which tools to upgrade first (and why)**, and ranks the **skills from easiest to hardest** with a note on whether each is truly **required** or **optional**.

## 1. What "Completing the Game" Means

Stardew Valley has no single "you win" screen — it has **three milestones** that together count as "finishing" it. This guide drives toward all three, fastest first:

| Milestone | What it is | Typical timing (efficient play) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Community Center** | Restore the town by completing Junimo bundles. The main "story" goal. Rewards include the **Greenhouse**, **bus to the Desert**, **minecarts**, and bridges. | End of Year 1 → mid Year 2 |
| **Grandpa's Evaluation (4 candles)** | The original "win condition." Grandpa scores your farm at the start of **Year 3** (re-checkable anytime with a **Diamond** on his Shrine). 12+ points = all 4 candles. | Year 2–3 |
| **Perfection (100%)** | The true endgame: an averaged score across 11 categories. Hitting 100% unlocks the **Summit cutscene** and **Statue of True Perfection** (a free Prismatic Shard every day). | Year 2–3+ |

> **Maxing all tools** (every tool to Iridium + the Mastery tools) is folded into the plan below, since you need the upgraded tools to reach Perfection efficiently anyway.

## 2. The Headline Strategy (read this first)

Everything in the fast route rests on **four pillars**:

1. **Cash crops → artisan goods.** Plant the most profitable seasonal crop, then convert harvests into **Wine/Juice (Kegs)** and **Pickles/Jelly (Preserves Jars)**. Artisan goods are where the real money is, and money buys tool upgrades, Obelisks, and the 10-million-gold Gold Clock.
2. **The Mines & Skull Cavern.** Ore is the gateway to *everything*: bars for tool upgrades, Iridium for the best gear, and progress toward Perfection. Push the Mines early.
3. **The Community Center.** Its rewards (Greenhouse, Desert bus, minecarts) directly accelerate the other two pillars. Prioritize it over the Joja route.
4. **Daily relationship + collection upkeep.** Talk to villagers, ship one of every item, donate to the Museum, and catch new fish as you go — these feed Perfection so you aren't grinding them all at the end.

> **Golden efficiency rule:** Never waste energy or a day. Use the **Spa** (free energy/health refill), sleep before passing out, and always carry seeds, food, and a few **bombs/staircases** for the mines.

## 3. Tool Upgrades — Costs, Order & the Winter Trick

### How upgrades work
Tools are upgraded by **Clint at the Blacksmith**. Each upgrade **takes 2 days** (the tool is gone during that time) and must be done **in order** (Copper → Steel → Gold → Iridium). You provide gold **and** metal bars:

| Tier | Gold cost | Bars needed |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Copper** | 2,000g | 5 Copper Bars |
| **Steel** | 5,000g | 5 Iron Bars |
| **Gold** | 10,000g | 5 Gold Bars |
| **Iridium** | 25,000g | 5 Iridium Bars |

**Upgradeable tools:** Pickaxe, Axe, Hoe, Watering Can, **Trash Can**, and the **Pan** (the Copper Pan you grab in the Mines can also be upgraded). The **Scythe is not upgradeable** (but see the Iridium Scythe under Mastery).

### Which tool to upgrade FIRST (and why)

For the fastest completion, prioritize tools that unlock **progress and ore**, not just convenience:

1. **Pickaxe → first.** Faster rock-breaking means more ore, faster Mines/Skull Cavern descent, and quicker access to geodes and Iridium — which feeds *every* other upgrade. This is the single best first investment.
2. **Hoe → second.** The area-of-effect upgrades let you till huge fields in one swing (Gold/Iridium = a **6×3, 18-tile** sweep), massively speeding up planting and artifact-spot digging.
3. **Axe → third.** You need the **Steel Axe** to clear the log blocking the **Secret Woods**, and lots of **Hardwood** to repair Willy's boat to **Ginger Island** (required for Perfection).
4. **Watering Can → upgrade in WINTER.** ⚠️ Because the tool is gone for 2 days, upgrading the Watering Can mid-season can kill un-watered crops. **Upgrade it in Winter when nothing is growing**, or once **Sprinklers** cover your whole farm so you don't hand-water at all.
5. **Trash Can / Pan → whenever convenient.** Minor quality-of-life (upgraded Trash Cans refund some gold). Low priority.

> **Beginner tip — skip hand-watering entirely:** Craft **Sprinklers** (Farming Lv2), **Quality Sprinklers** (Lv6), and **Iridium Sprinklers** (Lv9) as soon as you can. Sprinklers make the Watering Can almost irrelevant and free you to mine and fish instead.

### Watering Can & Hoe area-of-effect by tier
| Tier | Area of effect |
| --- | --- |
| Copper | 3 tiles in a line |
| Steel | 5 tiles in a line |
| Gold | 3×3 (9 tiles) |
| Iridium | **6×3 (18 tiles)** |

### Fishing Rods (bought from Willy, NOT the Blacksmith)
| Rod | Cost | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Training Rod | 25g | Easier minigame, only catches common fish |
| Bamboo Pole | 500g | Your standard starter rod |
| **Fiberglass Rod** | 1,800g | Requires **Fishing Lv2**; lets you use **bait** |
| **Iridium Rod** | 7,500g | Requires **Fishing Lv6**; lets you use **bait + tackle** |
| Advanced Iridium Rod | Mastery reward | Holds **two bobbers** at once (see below) |

### The "max tools" finish line — Mastery tools (1.6 feature)
After you hit **Level 10 in all five skills**, the **Mastery Cave** opens in Cindersap Forest. Spending Mastery Points there grants the ultimate tools:

- **Farming Mastery → Iridium Scythe** (harvests crops and is great for hay) + Statue of Blessings + Golden Animal Crackers (double an animal's produce).
- **Fishing Mastery → Advanced Iridium Rod** (two bobbers at once) + Challenge Bait + golden fishing chests.
- **Mining Mastery → Statue of the Dwarf King + Heavy Furnace**; gem rocks drop double gems.
- **Foraging Mastery → Mystic Tree Seed + Treasure Totem**; find Golden Mystery Boxes.
- **Combat Mastery → Anvil + Mini-Forge** + a new **trinket** equipment slot.

> **Is Mastery required to finish?** **No** — Mastery is *optional* for Perfection. But the Iridium Scythe and Advanced Iridium Rod are huge time-savers, and you'll be at level 10 in everything for Perfection anyway, so you'll unlock it naturally.

## 4. Skills — Easiest to Hardest + Needed vs Optional

There are **5 skills**, each leveled to 10. Every skill needs **15,000 total XP** to reach level 10, with profession choices at **Level 5** and **Level 10**.

### Ranked easiest → hardest to max
| # | Skill | Why this difficulty | How to level it fast |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Foraging** (easiest) | Almost passive — just pick things up and chop wood. | Gather all seasonal forage daily, chop trees, tap maple/oak/pine, and plant a **Mystic/large tree farm**. The **6 Mastery** chopping is huge XP. |
| 2 | **Farming** (very easy) | Planting and harvesting gives steady, reliable XP. | Plant **large fields** every season; harvesting hundreds of crops races you to 10. Caring for animals adds XP too. |
| 3 | **Mining** (easy–medium) | Just break rocks, but requires diving the Mines. | Break **every rock**; descend the Mines and **Skull Cavern**; gem/geode nodes give bonus XP. |
| 4 | **Combat** (medium) | Needs you to fight monsters, often while mining. | Clear monster floors in the Mines/Skull Cavern; the **Monster Eradication Goals** double as Perfection progress. |
| 5 | **Fishing** (hardest for beginners) | The fishing **minigame** is the steepest learning curve, though each catch gives big XP. | Fish daily; buy the **Fiberglass → Iridium Rod**; eat fishing-buff food; once you "get" the minigame this is actually the **fastest** XP per action. |

> **Fishing note:** It's the *hardest to learn* but the *fastest to grind* once you can hold the bobber on the fish. Practice with the **Training Rod** first if the minigame frustrates you.

### Is each skill actually needed?
| Skill | For Community Center + Grandpa 4 candles | For **Perfection (100%)** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Farming | Helpful (money), level not required | **Required** (all skills must reach Lv10 for "Farmer Level"; crops needed for shipping/cooking) |
| Mining | Helpful (ore for tools), level not required | **Required** (Lv10) + ore/gems for crafting & shipping |
| Foraging | A few bundle items, level not required | **Required** (Lv10) |
| Fishing | Fish bundles need fish, level not required | **Required** (Lv10) **and** you must **catch all 72 fish** |
| Combat | Not required | **Required** (Lv10) **and** complete all **12 Monster Eradication Goals** |

**Bottom line:**
- To just **beat the Community Center and get Grandpa's 4 candles**, *no skill is strictly required to be maxed* — you need items, money, and friendships, not skill levels.
- For **Perfection, all five skills to Level 10 are mandatory** (the "Farmer Level" category), and Fishing + Combat carry extra hard requirements (all fish, all monster goals). So if your goal is 100%, **no skill is optional.**

## 5. Season-by-Season Plan

A compressed, efficiency-first walkthrough. Adjust to your luck and energy, but this is the fastest sensible order.

### 🌱 Year 1 — Spring
**Goals:** establish cash flow, start the Mines, begin the Community Center, befriend key NPCs.
- **Day 1–2:** Plant your starting **Parsnips**. Clear debris around the farmhouse for space and wood/stone. Talk to every villager you meet (free friendship + the town opens up).
- **Forage daily:** Spring Onions (south of the farm), Daffodils, Leeks, Dandelions, Wild Horseradish — sell or gift them.
- **Egg Festival (Spring 13):** Buy as many **Strawberry seeds** as you can afford and plant them immediately — they're the season's best profit and keep producing.
- **The Mines (opens Spring 5):** Go most days. Break every rock, grab **Copper Ore**, kill slimes (Combat XP), and pick up the **Copper Pan** from the river cutscene. Aim to bank enough **Copper Bars** for your first **Pickaxe** upgrade.
- **Community Center (opens after the Spring cutscene):** Start dropping off easy **Spring Crops** and **Foraging** bundle items.
- **First tool upgrade:** As soon as you have 5 Copper Bars + 2,000g, upgrade the **Pickaxe** (or Hoe if you're going wide on crops).
- **Skills focus:** Farming (crops) + Mining/Combat (Mines) climb naturally; grab Foraging from daily pickups.

### ☀️ Year 1 — Summer
**Goals:** scale up money, build artisan infrastructure, push the Mines toward floor 120.
- **Best crops:** **Blueberries** (huge yield per seed) and **Starfruit** (highest base value — great for Wine). Plant a big field.
- **Build money machines:** Craft a wall of **Kegs** (Farming Lv8) and **Preserves Jars** (Farming Lv4). Turn Starfruit/Blueberries into **Wine** and **Jelly** — often 2–3× the raw value.
- **Tools:** Move toward **Steel** tools (Iron Bars). Upgrade the **Hoe** and **Axe**; clear the **Secret Woods** with the Steel Axe for Hardwood.
- **Mines:** Reach **floor 120** for the **Skull Key** (unlocks Skull Cavern). Grab the **Stardrop in the floor-100 treasure chest**.
- **Fishing:** Start seriously fishing; buy the **Fiberglass Rod** (Lv2). Begin logging new fish for the collection.
- **Community Center:** Knock out Summer crop/fish bundles.

### 🍂 Year 1 — Fall
**Goals:** maximize the richest season, finish most of the Community Center, prep Ginger Island materials.
- **Best crops:** **Cranberries** and **Pumpkins** (and **Sweet Gem Berry** from a **Rare Seed** bought at the Traveling Cart — it sells huge and feeds Old Master Cannoli later for a Stardrop).
- **Artisan goods:** Keep Kegs/Jars running non-stop — this is your biggest income window.
- **Vault bundles:** Pay off the **Vault** (gold bundles totaling **42,500g**) to repair the **bus to the Desert/Skull Cavern**.
- **Community Center:** Aim to complete the **Pantry** for the **Greenhouse** (year-round crops = endless Ancient Fruit/Starfruit Wine money) and the **Boiler Room** for **minecarts**.
- **Tools:** Begin **Gold** upgrades (Gold Bars). Save Iridium for later.
- **Spirit's Eve (Fall 27):** Grab a **Golden Pumpkin** if you can (a universal-loved gift + collectible).

### ❄️ Year 1 — Winter
**Goals:** the "downtime" season — perfect for upgrades, mining, fishing, Museum, and relationships.
- **No outdoor crops** (except the Greenhouse and Winter forage), so this is the **ideal time to upgrade your Watering Can** without losing harvests.
- **Mine hard:** With the bus repaired, dive **Skull Cavern** for **Iridium Ore** (use **bombs** and crafted **Staircases** to descend fast). Bank Iridium Bars for **Iridium tools**.
- **Museum:** Donate minerals/artifacts you've collected — completing it (95 items) grants a **Stardrop**.
- **Relationships:** Use the slow season to gift villagers up to high hearts (see the [Relationships manual](RELATIONSHIPS.md)).
- **Skills:** Push Fishing and Mining toward Lv10.

### 🌱 Year 2 — and beyond
**Goals:** finish the Community Center, reach Perfection, and lock in Grandpa's 4 candles.
- **Greenhouse engine:** Fill the Greenhouse (and later **Ginger Island farm**) with **Ancient Fruit** → **Wine** in Kegs. This is the classic path to the millions needed for the **Gold Clock** and **Obelisks**.
- **Ginger Island:** Repair **Willy's boat** (**200 Hardwood + 5 Iridium Bars + 5 Battery Packs**; 1,000g per trip). Then collect **Golden Walnuts** (130 total) and unlock **Qi's Walnut Room** with the **Perfection Tracker**.
- **Max all tools to Iridium**, and once all skills hit Lv10, claim **Mastery** rewards (Iridium Scythe, Advanced Iridium Rod, etc.).
- **Grandpa's Evaluation:** On **Spring 1, Year 3** Grandpa scores you; **12+ points = 4 candles**. If you're short, place a **Diamond** on his Shrine to be re-evaluated later. Four candles rewards the **Statue Of Perfection** (free Iridium Ore daily).
- **Grind out the Perfection categories** below.

## 6. The Perfection Checklist (true 100%)

Perfection is an **average of these 11 categories** (weights shown). View progress on the **Perfection Tracker** in Qi's Walnut Room (Ginger Island).

| Category | Requirement | Weight |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Produce & Forage Shipped** | Ship **one of every** crop/forage item (154) | **15%** |
| **Great Friends** | Max hearts with **all 34 villagers** (8 for datables, 10 for others). Includes **Kent** (Year 2), so impossible in Year 1 | **11%** |
| **Golden Clock on Farm** | Build the **Gold Clock** (**10,000,000g**) | **10%** |
| **Monster Slayer Hero** | Complete all **12** Adventurer's Guild eradication goals | **10%** |
| **Found All Stardrops** | Collect all **7** Stardrops | **10%** |
| **Cooking Recipes Made** | Cook every recipe (**81**) | **10%** |
| **Crafting Recipes Made** | Craft every item (**149**) | **10%** |
| **Fish Caught** | Catch every fish (**72**) | **10%** |
| **Farmer Level** | Reach **Level 10 in all 5 skills** (25 levels) | **5%** |
| **Golden Walnuts Found** | Find all **130** on Ginger Island | **5%** |
| **Obelisks on Farm** | Build all **4** Obelisks | **4%** |

### Knocking out the hard / expensive ones
- **The 7 Stardrops** (don't miss any):
  1. **Stardew Valley Fair** — buy for 2,000 star tokens (Fall festival).
  2. **Mines floor 100** treasure chest.
  3. **Spouse/roommate** at **12.5 hearts**.
  4. **Krobus** in the Sewers for **20,000g**.
  5. **Old Master Cannoli** in the Secret Woods — give him a **Sweet Gem Berry** (grown from a Rare Seed).
  6. **Master Angler** — a letter from **Willy** after you catch **all 72 fish**.
  7. **Museum** — donate all **95** items.
- **The big money sinks** (plan your Wine empire around these):
  - **Gold Clock: 10,000,000g** (the single biggest hurdle — 10% of Perfection by itself).
  - **Earth Obelisk: 500,000g** + 10 Iridium Bars + 10 Earth Crystal.
  - **Water Obelisk: 500,000g** + materials.
  - **Desert Obelisk: 1,000,000g** + 20 Iridium Bars + 10 Coconut + 10 Cactus Fruit.
  - **Island Obelisk: 1,000,000g** + 10 Iridium Bars + 10 Dragon Tooth + 10 Banana.
  - Obelisks are bought from the **Wizard** after the **Goblin Problem** quest (Witch's Swamp).
- **Great Friends:** start gifting early and steadily; **Kent returns in Year 2**, so this category can't finish in Year 1. See the [Relationships manual](RELATIONSHIPS.md) for exact loved gifts.
- **Fish Caught (72):** some fish are season/weather/location-locked — keep a checklist and fish in every season. Legendary fish need high Fishing skill.
- **Golden Walnuts (130):** explore all of Ginger Island, solve its puzzles, and complete Qi's challenges.

### After Perfection
Hitting 100% triggers the **Summit cutscene**, a parrot flyover, and the **Statue Of True Perfection** (interact with the Perfection Tracker) — it drops a **Prismatic Shard every day**. Golden Chickens/Eggs and a secret hat also unlock.

## 7. Endgame Order of Operations

A clean priority list once the early game is rolling:

1. **Repair tools up the chain** (Pickaxe → Hoe → Axe → Watering Can in winter), reaching **Gold** by end of Year 1.
2. **Finish the Community Center** (Greenhouse + Desert bus are the priority rewards).
3. **Build a Wine empire** (Greenhouse + Ginger Island Ancient Fruit → Kegs).
4. **Dive Skull Cavern** for Iridium → **Iridium tools** + Obelisk/boat materials.
5. **Catch all 72 fish** and **complete all 12 Monster goals** (these gate two Stardrops/Perfection categories).
6. **Max all 5 skills → unlock Mastery** → grab the Iridium Scythe & Advanced Iridium Rod.
7. **Collect all 7 Stardrops** and **finish the Museum (95)**.
8. **Ship one of everything (154)**, **cook all 81**, **craft all 149**.
9. **Buy the 4 Obelisks** and grind to the **10M Gold Clock**.
10. **Collect 130 Golden Walnuts**, max all 34 friendships → **Perfection**.
11. Place a **Diamond** on Grandpa's Shrine if you still need the **4-candle** evaluation.
